🌟 Active Events During Your Week (Jun 13–20)

🎵 Les Francos de Montréal
Jun 12–20 · ALL WEEK
World's largest French-language music festival — 150+ performances over 9 days. FREE outdoor mega-stage at Place des Festivals every night from ~8 PM. Ticketed indoor shows at MTELUS, Le Studio TD, and Place des Arts. 2026 lineup: Cœur de Pirate (Jun 19), Pierre Lapointe (Jun 18), Lorie (Jun 17), Laurent Voulzy, Orelsan, Lou-Adriane Cassidy, Émile Bilodeau and more. Street performers, food vendors, thousands of Montrealers every evening. The free outdoor stage is the main recommendation for the whole family — go every night.
Free outdoor stage nightlyTicketed indoor showsAll ages
🎪 Cirque du Soleil ECHO — Old Port Big Top
May 23 – Jun 28 · BOOK NOW
Cirque du Soleil was founded in Montreal in 1984. ECHO returns to the iconic Big Top at the Old Port through June 28. Full ~2.5-hour show: aerialists, acrobats, live original music, stunning staging. Pregnancy-safe (fully seated). Perfect for all ages. Book NOW at cirquedusoleil.com. Summer weeknights sell out weeks ahead. Planned for Tuesday June 16 in the itinerary.
Ticketed — BOOK NOWAll agesPregnancy safe
🎨 MURAL Festival — Freshly Painted Murals
Murals permanent · Festival ended Jun 14
The MURAL Festival (Jun 4–14) has just finished painting enormous murals along Boulevard Saint-Laurent — you arrive the day before the festival ends, so everything is at peak freshness. Free self-guided walk from Sherbrooke to Mont-Royal Ave. TourBird free audio guide available. Guided tours from Spade & Palacio (~$20). 100+ murals. Flat terrain throughout — perfect for Danielle and Lynda.
FreeOutdoorAll ages · flat terrain
🥁 Tam-Tams Drum Circle — Base of Mont Royal
Every Sunday · Jun 14 + Jun 21
Every Sunday in summer, a massive spontaneous drum circle erupts at the George-Étienne Cartier Monument — hundreds of drummers, dancers, fire jugglers, and Montrealers of every description. Zero admission, zero agenda. Starts ~11 AM on sunny Sundays. Combine with the Mont Royal hike on Sunday June 14 — it's right at the base of the mountain trail.
Totally freeOutdoor · Sunday only
🎭 Montreal Fringe Festival
Jun 11–21 · Overlaps your trip
Independent theatre with free outdoor programming at Fringe Park (corner Rachel + Saint-Laurent, steps from the Plateau). Live bands, street performers, shows in English and French. Easy to pop into on any Plateau afternoon. The Drag Race marquee event happens June 20 — your departure day, so probably missed, but the outdoor park programming is free all week.
Free outdoorSome ticketed shows

🗺️ City Layout in One Paragraph

Montreal is an island. Downtown is center-south. Old Montreal + Old Port is at the southern waterfront — start every day here. The Plateau-Mont-Royal is northeast of downtown — the best neighborhood. Mile End is north of the Plateau. Olympic Park is east. The Lachine Canal and Atwater Market are west of downtown. Mont Royal (the mountain) is literally in the center of the island. Almost everything in this guide is 15–20 minutes from downtown by metro. Stay downtown or Old Montreal and you can walk to most things or take one metro stop.
